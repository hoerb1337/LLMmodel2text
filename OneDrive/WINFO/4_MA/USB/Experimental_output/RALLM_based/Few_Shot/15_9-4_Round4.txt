This event is a type of start event that initiates a process after a specific time duration or at a specific point in time. The first task of the "Coordination Unit" is to create a meeting folder. After the meeting folder is created, the "Coordination Unit" proceeds to upload all relevant documents. 

A week before the meeting, a timer intermediate catch event "1 Week Before Meeting" is activated. This type of intermediate event waits for a specific duration of time. After the timer event, the "Coordination Unit" sends a reminder to the "Committee Members" to read the documentation through an intermediate throw event named "Remind Members to Read Documentation". This event is used to show where messages are sent within the process. 

After sending the reminder, the "Coordination Unit" attends the meeting and prepares the meeting minutes. The meeting minutes are then subjected to a quality assurance check by the "Coordination Unit". If the quality assurance check takes more than 5 days, a timer boundary event named "5 days" is triggered, prompting a reminder to the "Coordination Unit". 

Following the quality assurance check, the "Coordination Unit" sends the minutes to the "Committee Members" through an intermediate throw event named "Send Minutes To Members". The process then concludes with an end event.

On the side of the "Committee Members", they receive the meeting reminder through an intermediate catch event named "Meeting Reminder". The meeting then takes place. After the meeting, they wait for the meeting minutes through an intermediate throw event named "Waiting On Meeting Minutes". Once they receive the minutes through an intermediate catch event named "Send Minutes to Members", their part of the process concludes with an end event.
